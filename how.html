How FlyLoop Works

Imagine you’re the customer. Instead of saving once a year and hunting for flights, you treat travel like Netflix: a predictable subscription that quietly builds your next escape in the background.

Your journey as a member

  • 1. You land on the site.
    You see three membership tiers with clear prices and destinations. You pick the one that matches how often you want to travel.
  • 2. You create an account & choose a plan.
    After clicking Join, you would normally sign up, add your card, and confirm your subscription. From that moment, your “travel credits” or eligibility start building.
  • 3. Your subscription runs quietly each month.
    Instead of one big, painful payment, a small monthly amount goes toward your trips. Think: “$49/month” instead of “$900 at once.”
  • 4. You log in to your dashboard.
    Inside your account, you’d see your plan, how many trips you’re entitled to, and which destinations are currently available.
  • 5. You browse available trip windows.
    The platform shows you future dates and destinations that match your plan: LA in March, Montréal in April, Cancun in October, etc.
  • 6. You redeem a trip.
    When you’re ready, you choose a destination and date. FlyLoop (in a real version) would then handle the flight booking, hotel, and basic transfers.
  • 7. You receive your itinerary.
    A few days later, you get your flight details, hotel confirmation, and a simple suggested itinerary so you don’t have to overthink the trip.
  • 8. You travel.
    You fly, stay, explore, and return knowing that your subscription is still running—quietly building your next escape.
  • 9. You manage your membership.
    You can view upcoming eligibility, see past trips, and—if this was live— request changes, pauses, upgrades, or plan switches from your account.

Behind the scenes (for your portfolio)

This page acts like a mini product spec. From a business and tech point of view, this flow implies:

  • Recurring billing & subscription management (Stripe, etc.).
  • A “credits” or eligibility system tied to plan + time.
  • An inventory/calendar of flights and hotels you control.
  • A booking engine that locks in trips once redeemed.
  • Rules for cancellations, pauses, and minimum commitment.

You can show this page in a pitch, CV, or portfolio to prove the idea isn’t just a pretty UI — it has a clear customer journey and real business logic behind it.
